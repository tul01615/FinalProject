Spatial Distribution

This map shows the importance of visual hierarchy and proper layout. Throughout this lab, different features on the symbology tab were explored. The type of symbology used depends greatly on the data that is being displayed. For spatial distribution, it is best to use a choropleth map.

For this map specifically, the Hispanic population should normalized with the overall population of the United States. It is best to display the data in percentages, making it easy to read the data within the map.

Homicides vs. Airbnbs in Chicago

The problem at hand when creating these maps was to describe the spatial distribution of the density of homicides by police district and compare this spatial distribution to Airbnb listings and prices in Chicago. In order to accomplish this, joins had to be created to merge standalone data with spatial data. The data also had to be summarized to determine the frequency needed to map density. Using the area and the frequency, the density for both homicides and Airbnb's can be calculated separately. The maps show that not many Airbnb's are in areas within high-density homicide districts.

Poisoned Water in Flint, Michigan

From the given data, the locations where people are most at risk for drinking poisoned water had to be determined. There were three conditions to follow; the areas must be within 0.3 miles of a TRI facility with water pollution over 10,000 tons, within 0.3 miles of a river, and within 0.2 miles with water service lines made of lead.

For the first condition, the standalone table must be joined with the spatial data in order to begin. Attribute selections can then be made to only select the facilities with water pollution over 10,000 tons. Using the new data, a buffer can be created 0.3 miles from one of these locations, making sure to dissolve all.

For the second condition, all water data can be clipped so that only the water ways within Flint are visible. The only river in Flint is Flint River so we can select by attribute to only select Flint River and export that data into a new attribute table. From here a new 0.3 mile buffer can be completed.

For the third condition, another attribute selection can be made so that only the lead is selected and exported into a new table. Another buffer can be made for 0.2 miles from any service line made of lead.

The areas reminding show where people are most at risk of suffering from drinking poisonous water.
